Highlights


Boeing

  • July 7 1925 Model 40 Boeing takes its first flight. It was designed to carry mail and is the company’s first passenger plane.

  • May 6 1930 Boeing partners with Pratt & Whitney to create a huge aerospace conglomerate called United Aircraft and Transportation Corporation.

  • Feb 8 1933 Model 247 Boeing takes its first flights as the first modern airliner, however commercial air travel is still dominated by Douglas’ DC-2 and DC-3.

  • Sept 18 1934 United Aircraft and Transportation Corporation splits into three companies that are still fully functional today: Boeing, United Technologies Corporation, and United Airlines.

  • July 28 1935 Model 299 (B-17) takes its first flight.

  • March 6 1944 Sixty engineers from Boeing hold SPEEA’s first meeting.

  • August 6 1945 A B-29 model drops an atomic bomb on Hiroshima.

  • July 27 1949 The De Havilland Comet, the first commercial jetliner, makes its first flight.

  • April 13 1966 Boeing plans to build the 747, the first wide-body jetliner.

  • September 26 1981 First flight of the 767.

  • March 28 1990 The model 737 becomes the world’s best-selling jetliner.

  • December 15 2009 First flight of the 787.

  • January 29 2016 First flight of the 737 MAX.
