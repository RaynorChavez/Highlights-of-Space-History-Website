Teleporting Photons from Earth to Space

Using a process called quantum entanglement, a team of Chinese researches were able to successfully teleport millions of photons is a span of 32 days from a ground station in Tibet to a low orbiting Micius satellite.

Previous teleportation experiments have also been done successfully but not at this great a distance. To ensure that quantum entanglement had indeed occurred, the scientist measured the photons to avoid any wrong information that may affect the data.

Scientists will be able to use the information between two entangled particles especially in internet network services, which will see information being coded in entangled particles instead of in binary, which consists of 1s and 0s. A satellite launched in 2016 is able to detect the quantum state of photons beamed from the ground and this may aid research to produce the first ground-to-satellite uplink for long distance teleportation. This will surely be a large step forwards towards global-scale quantum internet.
